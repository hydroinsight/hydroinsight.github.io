## Overview

This project focuses on improving ERA5 snow depth estimates using machine learning and auxiliary environmental datasets.

The main objective is to enhance the spatial and predictive performance of ERA5 snow depth data by integrating remote sensing products, topographic variables, and ground-based observations.

---

## Research Focus

Snow depth is an important variable for hydrology, water resources, climate studies, and mountain catchment analysis. However, reanalysis datasets such as ERA5 may contain biases and spatial limitations, especially in complex terrain.

This project develops a data-driven downscaling and bias-correction framework to improve snow depth estimation.

---

## Methods

The project uses a machine learning-based framework including:

- Data preprocessing and quality control
- Feature engineering from topographic and environmental variables
- Model training and validation
- Bias correction and downscaling
- Spatial performance evaluation

---

## Data

The analysis may include:

- ERA5 snow depth data
- Remote sensing products

- Ground observation data
- Climate and environmental predictors

## Expected Outputs

The project is designed to generate:
