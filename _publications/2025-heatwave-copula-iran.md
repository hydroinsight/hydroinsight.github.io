## Overview

This study develops a copula-based framework to assess heatwave extremes and the probability of their simultaneous occurrence in Iran under climate change.

The research investigates temporal and spatial changes in three key heatwave characteristics:

- Frequency
- Duration
- Severity

The analysis covers Iran over the period **1972–2023** using ERA5 reanalysis temperature data.

## Research Focus

Heatwaves are multidimensional hydroclimatic extreme events. Their impacts are not determined by a single variable, but by the simultaneous behavior of multiple characteristics such as frequency, duration, and severity.

This study addresses the following questions:

- How have heatwave frequency, duration, and severity changed across Iran?
- Are these characteristics increasing simultaneously?
- What is the probability of compound heatwave extremes?
- Which heatwave characteristics show the strongest increasing trends?
- How can copula functions improve the probabilistic assessment of heatwave risk?

---

## Data and Methods

The study uses ERA5 reanalysis temperature data for Iran from **1972 to 2023**.

The main methodological components include:

- Heatwave identification using the 95th percentile threshold
- Trend analysis using the Mann–Kendall test
- Temporal change-point assessment
- Spatial analysis of heatwave trends
- Probability density function analysis
- Copula-based joint probability modeling

Four copula functions were evaluated:

- Gaussian copula
- Clayton copula
- Frank copula
- Gumbel copula

---

## Main Findings

The study shows that heatwaves in Iran have become more frequent, longer, and more severe.

Key findings include:

- Heatwave frequency, duration, and severity show significant increasing trends.
- In many regions, changes began around **1998–2002**.
- The current average number of heatwaves in Iran is about **18 events per year**.
- Heatwave frequency has increased by approximately **60%** compared to the beginning of the study period.
- Heatwave severity has increased, with average temperatures exceeding the threshold by about **1.8°C**.
- Copula results show a high probability of simultaneous extreme heatwave characteristics.

---

## Compound Heatwave Risk

The copula-based analysis indicates that extreme heatwave characteristics can occur simultaneously.

The results show a high likelihood of compound heatwave conditions, especially when:

- Frequency exceeds **20 events per year**
- Severity is above **1.5°C**
- Duration is **7 days or longer**

This highlights the increasing probability of intense and prolonged heatwaves in Iran.

---

## Scientific Contribution

This research contributes to climate risk assessment by combining spatial-temporal heatwave trend analysis with copula-based probabilistic modeling.

The study provides a framework for understanding compound heatwave extremes and supports future climate adaptation, early warning systems, water resource planning, and environmental risk assessment in arid and semi-arid regions.
